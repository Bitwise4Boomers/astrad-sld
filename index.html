Reading the drawing: Zone A is a conventional IEC 60617 power single line — incomer through transformer, main LT bus, breaker-protected feeders, the UPS room (rectifier/charger, battery bank, inverter, static bypass), and final distribution boards. Zone B continues the single-line convention (one line per path, arrows for direction) into the radar's RF/signal/data chain, which has no standard power-system symbol set of its own. The triangular markers at the base of zone B mark where each load actually receives its electrical supply from zone A.

Engineering note: Transformer rating (100 kVA), breaker ratings, battery autonomy (30 min), and DG capacity are typical values for a facility of this load size — not ASTRAD's published electrical specification, which is not publicly available. Verify against site documentation or the facility's actual single-line drawing before using for installation or protection coordination work. The TRM/OCXO/CSG/ADPT block names and the 588-element, 12-cluster array structure are drawn from the published ASTRAD literature (see footer).
Power-chain symbols and topology follow standard IEC 60617 single-line drafting practice for a small instrumentation facility; exact switchgear ratings are typical/illustrative, not ASTRAD's as-built electrical spec (not publicly published).
